Bulgaria's History

Discover Bulgaria's vibrant history, from ancient civilizations to modern times. Explore its diverse cultural tapestry and resilient spirit that continues to shape the country's narrative.

Bulgaria Through the Ages

  • VI-V BCE

    First settlements

    The history of Sofia was significantly influenced by the existence of hot mineral springs. The initial settlements in the contemporary Sofia region were established by prehistoric people who were attracted by the geological features of the area. In the 8th century BCE, the Thracian tribe of Serdi settled in the region before becoming part of the Roman Empire.

  • 29 BCE

    Roman Rule

    The Romans conquered Sofia around 29 BCE, renaming the city 'Serdica'. In the 2nd century AD, the first Roman baths were built around the main hot spring. Today, Sofia proudly presents its rich historical legacy by providing residents and visitors free access to hot mineral water in the city centre.

  • 1382-1878 CE

    Ottoman Rule

    During the Ottoman-Bulgarian War, the city succumbed to the Turks, leading to a period of Ottoman rule. Under their dominion, mosques and minarets dominated the skyline, giving a distinct Oriental character to the city. However, the situation changed when Bulgaria was liberated from Ottoman control following the Russo-Turkish Wars (1877-78). A year after liberation, Sofia was designated as the capital of the new country, sparking rapid growth as it transitioned from a provincial town to a bustling European capital.

  • 1945-1990 CE

    Soviet Rule

    After the Communist party took power, extensive construction projects reshaped Sofia following heavy bombing during WWII. The city was embellished with imposing buildings and Communist monuments. The Communist era ended on November 10, 1989, marking a turning point. Despite facing challenges in the 1990s, Sofia underwent a remarkable transformation into a modern European city, particularly following Bulgaria's accession to the European Union in 2007.
