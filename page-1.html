  • VI-V millennium BCE

    Prehistory

    One of these settlements can still be seen in the Sofia History Museum. Prehistoric people decided to settle in today's territory of Sofia attracted by its hot mineral springs.

  • 29 BCE

    Roman Rule

    Around 29 BCE, Sofia became part of the Roman Empire. During this time, the city expanded and several buildings were erected, such as public baths, basilicas, and amphitheaters.

  • 809 CE

    Byzantine Empire

    During the reign of Khan Krum, Sofia was incorporated into the First Bulgarian Empire and changed name to Sredets.

  • 1382-1878

    Ottomans Rule

    During the Ottoman-Bulgarian War, the city fell to the Turks. Under the Ottomans, mosques and its minarets dominated the skyline giving the city a distinctive Oriental appearance.
