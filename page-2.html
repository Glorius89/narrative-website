Religious Buildings

Sofia's skyline narrates a rich cultural tale through its diverse religious landmarks. From the iconic Alexander Nevsky Cathedral to the ancient St. Sofia Church and the Ottoman-inspired Banya Bashi Mosque, each structure contributes to the city's unique tapestry.

Historical Buildings

Sofia's historical buildings embody a captivating fusion of tradition and modernity, offering a glimpse into the city's enduring legacy. Walk the city's streets to encounter the stately National Assembly, the Presidential Palace, and the transformed Royal Palace, now the National Art Gallery.

Art Galleries

Sofia's cultural tapestry unfolds in its museums and art galleries. The National Palace of Culture buzzes with creativity, while the National Art Gallery and Sofia City Art Gallery provide insights into Bulgaria's artistic journey.

ST. NEVSKY CATHEDRAL

Alexander Pomerantsev

One of Bulgaria's main tourist destinations is the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, one of the biggest Eastern Orthodox cathedrals in the world, which is situated in Sofia. The cathedral was named for Saint Alexander Nevsky, a Russian nobleman who lived in the early 13th century, and was constructed in memory of Russian soldiers who lost their lives during the Russo-Turkish War of 1877, which resulted in Bulgaria's freedom from Ottoman domination. Although the structure was started in 1882, it wasn't finished until 1912. The cathedral's 150-foot-tall central dome and its 175-foot-tall bell down are its two most notable features.

  • Location: Pl. "Sveti Aleksandar Nevski", 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Mon-Sun 7am-7pm
  • Architectural Style: Neo-Byzantine architecture

BANYA BASHI MOSQUE

Mimar Sinan

The only mosque in Sofia that is still in operation is Banya Bashi Mosque. The finest Ottoman architect, Mimar Sinan, who also designed the Blue Mosque in Istanbul and the Sultan Selim Mosque in Edirne, created the design for it in 1576. This charming mosque is a prime example of Ottoman architecture, bringing vibrancy to the heart of Sofia. The term Banya Bashi, which translates to "many baths," comes from the nearby Tsentralnata Banya (Central Baths). The word for bath in Bulgarian is "banya."

  • Location: Knyagina Maria Luisa Blvd 18, 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Mon-Sun 6am-10pm
  • Architectural Style: Ottoman architecture

ST. GEORGE ROTUNDA

Constantine the Great

Constructed around the 4th century AD by Constantine the Great, this cylindrical building was initially meant for public use but was subsequently converted into a Christian baptistery. Under Emperor Justinian's reign, it was converted into a church and, during the Ottoman era, became Gyul Dzhamasi. It was used as a mausoleum following Bulgaria's freedom in 1878 and was later repaired in 1915. St. George Rotunda is still the most ancient and venerated active temple in Sofia, holding daily services with Byzantine music.

  • Location: Knyaz Alexander Dondukov Blvd 2, 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Mon-Sun 8am-7:30pm
  • Architectural Style: Early Christian architecture

Ancient Serdica Complex

Thracian tribe Serdi

The Thracian Serdi tribe founded Serdika 2,000 years ago BC. Under Roman rule, Serdika developed into Ulpia Serdika, which Emperor Constantine the Great favoured as his own Rome. Beneath Sofia's Presidential underpass lay the remnants of this strategically important city, once defended by high walls and studded with opulent palaces. Serdika's rich past is reflected in its mediaeval alleys and intact eastern gate, which were designated as historical and archaeological reserves in 1976.

  • Location: Pl. "Nezavisimost", 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Mon-Sun 10am-6pm
  • Architectural Style: Roman/Byzantine

Saint Sofia Statue

Georgi Chapkanov

Sveta Sofia, who was erected in 2000 to take the place of a statue of Lenin that had been taken down ten years earlier, towers over Bulgaria's representative centre with her arms extended. She has golden skin and dark eyes. Despite being named after Sveta Sofia, the statue does not really feature a single person. She is actually a composite of several figures. The two who are most frequently credited are Athena and Sofia, both of Hellenistic descent. Both represent "Holy Wisdom" (also known as "Hagia Sofia," the phrase for which the city and its founding church were named), and the statue has elements from that idea that date back centuries, such as the owl, the laurel wreath, and the crown.

  • Location: 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: 24/7
  • Architectural Style: Romanesque

Tzar Liberator Monument

Arnoldo Zocchi

The Tsar Liberator Monument, a 12-meter-tall neoclassical homage to Russian Emperor Alexander II, is located in Sofia's National Assembly Square. It was built in 1907 by Italian artist Arnoldo Zocchi as a sign of appreciation for Alexander II's assistance in freeing Bulgaria from Ottoman domination. The monument, which is made of polished black granite, shows the goddess Nike/Victoria leading a group of Russian and Bulgarian soldiers. Generals Gourko and Skobelev are surrounded by Grand Duke Nicholas Nicolaievich. Launched following the Russo-Turkish war, this cultural gem carries a sense of historical significance as it embodies a crucial period in Sofia's history.

  • Location: Pl. "Narodno sabranie", 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: 24/7
  • Architectural Style: Neoclassical

National Art Gallery

Friedrich Schwanberg

With a collection that spans a millennium, the National Gallery for Foreign Art in Sofia is a singular cultural institution in the Balkans. Located in a historically significant restored structure, the gallery showcases a wide range of artworks from far-off places, exotic tribes, and countries, contrasting with contemporary European styles. The gallery features approximately 10,000 museum pieces, including graphic works, paintings, and sculptures, with a focus on Christian sculpture from the Indian province of Goa, including the unique Poetic Reality collection. Experts from Bulgaria have transformed the gallery's architecture to include grand halls, multimedia features, and contemporary amenities, transforming it into an adaptable space for social, commercial, and cultural activities.

  • Location: Pl. "Knyaz Aleksandar I" 1, 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Tue-Sun 10am-6pm
  • Architectural Style: Neoclassical

Sofia History Museum

Petko Momchilov

The Central Mineral Baths building now houses the Sofia History Museum, which is focused on the economy, culture, ethnography, and life in Sofia and its environs. The museum's collection, which includes the Research Library and Photo Archives, totals about 120,000 items and spans the time from the sixth millennium BC to the 1940s. Eight thematic rooms showcasing the collection are: Antiquity, The Strength of the Spirit, Links with European Dynasties, A Sofia Street, State and Municipal Institutions, Sofia Home and Clothes, Culture and Recreation. In two other rooms, the Sofia History Museum hosts temporary exhibitions as well.

  • Location: 1 Banski Sq, Platno, Banski" 1, 1000 Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Tue-Sun 10am-6pm
  • Architectural Style: Byzantine and Neo-Baroque

Vazov National Theatre

Ferdinand Fellner and Hermann Helmer

The oldest and most prestigious theatre in Bulgaria is the "Ivan Vazov" National Theatre, which has a long history deeply ingrained in tradition while still making a major contribution to modernity. With a wide range of plays from modern to classical, the theatre appeals to a wide audience. It welcomes artistic endeavours that are original and places a significant emphasis on fostering national playwriting. The Art Nouveau-styled theatre, designed by Ferdinand Fellner and Hermann Helmer, is still recognisable in Sofia even after it was completed in 1906. The theatre, which has 848 seats, was inaugurated on January 3, 1907, and is adorned with magnificent artwork by Rudolf Fuchs, a Viennese artist.

  • Location: Dyakon Ignatiy St 5, 1000 Sofia Center, Sofia, Bulgaria
  • Opening Hours: Mon-Sun 7am-10:30pm
  • Architectural Style: Neoclassical
