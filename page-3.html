FOOD

Discover the essence of Bulgaria through its diverse cuisine, a delightful blend of tradition and innovation.

Bulgarian Food Classics

Tarator

Cold Soup

Tarator, a popular cold soup in Bulgaria, is a refreshing yoghurt-based beverage enjoyed as a summer refresher and commonly consumed at the beginning of meals. Legend suggests the recipe was borrowed from the Greeks and adapted by Bulgarians with the addition of water. This light and cold soup, made with yoghurt, cucumbers, and garlic, is particularly delightful during the hot summer months.

Meshana Skara

Mixed Grill

The Meshana Skara is a revered Bulgarian dish, particularly for meat enthusiasts. This mixed grill features classic items like kebapche, kyufte, pork skewers, and a pork steak. The dish, which includes one of each element, is served with French fries, bean salad with chopped onions, and lyutenitsa, making it a classic and satisfying Bulgarian meal.

Shopska Salad

Bulgarian Roast Pepper And Tomato Salad

Bulgaria's iconic Shopska salad, a culinary symbol, blends diced tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and peppers with grated sirene cheese and parsley. Its vibrant colors mirror the national flag, symbolizing a strong connection to the country. Named after the thrifty 'Shopi' people in Sofia and the mountains, this salad carries both international acclaim and cultural significance.

Banitsa

Baked extravaganza

Found in bakeries, coffee shops, canteens, and bus stations throughout Bulgaria, this traditional pastry is a delectable treat. Its addictive blend of buttery goodness and cheese is crafted by layering filo pastry dough, known as "fini kori" in Bulgarian, with butter and traditional Bulgarian cheese, then baked to a golden crust. Enjoy this delightful snack with a glass of ayran or boza; in any of its variations, Banitsa remains an integral component of a traditional Bulgarian breakfast.
